Kernel Booting

Contents

start_kernel() in init/main.c is the entry point into the booting kernel. Eventually, after initializing a bunch of stuff, at the end of start_kernel, after transforming into the hand-crafted init process, rest_init() is called.

rest_init() starts the kernel_init thread and kthreadd -- which is a thread that is used to start new threads -- and then calls kernel_init(). kernel_init() calls eventually init_post() which tries to execute -- kernel_execve -- /init, /sbin/init, /etc/init, /bin/init, or /bin/sh, in that order.
