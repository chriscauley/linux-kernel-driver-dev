Kernel Booting

Contents

start_kernel() in init/main.c is the entry point into the booting kernel. Eventually, after initializing a bunch of stuff, at the end of start_kernel, after transforming into the hand-crafted init process, rest_init() is called.

rest_init() starts the kernel_init thread and kthreadd -- which is a thread that is used to start new threads. kernel_init() tries to execute an init program via a series of calls to run_init_process, which relies on do_execve (in fs/exec.c) to do the execution. It will attempt to execute /sbin/init, /etc/init, /bin/init, or /bin/sh, in that order.
